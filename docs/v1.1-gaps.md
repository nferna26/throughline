# Throughline v1.1 — gap backlog (persona audit)

> Generated 2026-06-07 from a 5-persona, read-only end-to-end audit run after the
> v1.0 UX gaps (2–6) were closed. **33 gaps** found. Each persona walked the full
> loop — discover/import → plan → daily read → margin tutor → notes → export →
> next-day return — against the code and the self-verify screenshots
> (`e2e/shots/`). Nothing here is fixed yet; this is the prioritized backlog.
>
> Already shipped this run (not re-listed): first-run privacy promise, export
> preflight, plain-language lens names, a11y floor, recovery-when-behind,
> slide-to-collapse margin.

## Top of the backlog — five epics (synthesis)

The 33 findings cluster into five epics. Both gaps you flagged are confirmed and
land in the top two.

### A. Plan lifecycle management — **TOP (blocker)** · your "multiple plans" gap, confirmed
A reader can create multiple plans for the same book, with **no UI to view,
switch, pause, abandon, or delete** them, and **no prompt** when re-planning or
re-importing an already-planned book — so plans silently pile up and a returning
reader can't tell which is "live." Personas 2 (researcher, multiple books) and 5
(lapsed reader) both hit this; the researcher rated it a blocker.
**Ship:** a Plans surface (per book: status · progress · pace; actions resume /
pause / re-pace / delete) **+** a "This book already has a plan — Resume / Replace
/ Cancel" dialog on new-plan creation and on re-import.

### B. AI model & cost control — **major** · your "model picker" gap, confirmed
There is **no way to choose the model** in cloud mode; Anthropic defaults to
**Opus** (not cost-optimal — Sonnet is better and cheaper for this task), the
model isn't chosen at key setup, and there's **no usage/cost visibility**.
**Ship:** a model picker in Settings **and** AiSetupSheet (sensible defaults —
Sonnet for Anthropic), a one-line "you pay your provider per use" note, and
which-model-was-used in AI history.

### C. Cloud opt-in trust clarity — **major**
The local-only posture is genuinely solid, but the **first** cloud opt-in lacks a
prominent confirmation, the disclosure/destination isn't explicit enough, and the
Settings "stays on this Mac" statement can mislead once a cloud provider is on.
**Ship:** an explicit one-time "this sends your selection to <provider> under your
key" confirmation + make the Settings trust line reflect the active provider.

### D. Notes & export for serious note-takers — **major**
No cross-book or full-text note **search**; export Markdown lacks rich
**locators + source metadata** for Obsidian/Zotero; no tags/collections; session
metadata isn't surfaced. **Ship:** note search (within-book first, then
cross-book), richer export frontmatter (book/source/locator/citation), and a
tags/collection affordance.

### E. Reading-loop polish — **mixed (2 major, rest minor)**
The **finishing moment goes silent** (no celebration / next steps / notes review)
and the **teaser is hidden on a plan_ready book** — both major emotional beats for
a deliberate reader. The rest are minors: "go deeper" can scroll off-screen,
margin scroll doesn't reset on section change, and forecast/recovery copy could be
clearer.

## Full findings (by persona)

### Deliberate Classics/Philosophy Reader

**Finished book has no celebration or clear next steps** — `major` · effort M
- *What breaks:* When a reader finishes a book (pace.kind === 'done'), Today shows only 'You finished the book.' with no affordance to export notes, review the book, celebrate, or proceed to finding another. The reader must manually navigate to BookSwitcher to get the 'Find another book' option. This breaks the final emotional beat of completing a long, deliberate reading—the app goes silent instead of honoring the achievement.
- *Fix:* When pace.kind === 'done' and section === null, render a dedicated finished-book card that includes: (1) a brief celebration (e.g., 'You finished X by Y'), (2) key stats (chapters/sections read, notes captured, days elapsed), (3) a 'Review notes' link to jump to the Notebook, (4) an explicit 'Find another book' CTA. Optional: offer a one-click export of the entire notebook to Markdown (not just auto-export on save).
- *Evidence:* src/screens/Today.tsx:160-165 renders a bare label; no finished-book state handler. The 'Find another book' button (line 202) is always rendered below recovery, so it's not prominent when the book is done. The recap (TextReader.tsx:1162) shows 'You've reached the last section. Beautifully done.' in the reader, but onExit() simply returns to Today, which then shows a ghosted state.

**No visible indicator that plan will be rebalanced on next read after getting behind** — `minor` · effort S
- *What breaks:* A reader who falls behind sees the recovery panel on Today (09-recovery.png shows 'Behind · 6 days'), but the copy for 'ExtendFinish' option says 'Re-pace to finish by DATE. Adds X days; recomputes the daily plan.' There is no UI hint BEFORE choosing that option that (a) the plan WILL auto-rebalance if extended, or (b) what 'recomputes the daily plan' means—does it change tomorrow's assignment? Does it affect the whole book? A deliberate reader needs this clarity before committing.
- *Fix:* Add a help icon or expand the 'ExtendFinish' description to say: 'Re-pace to finish by DATE. Adds X days; tomorrow's reading assignment will be recalculated to distribute remaining sections across the new finish date. Completed sections stay completed.' Optional: show a preview of the new daily section count (e.g., 'will need ~2 sections/day instead of 2.5').
- *Evidence:* src/screens/Today.tsx:56-75 describeOption() for RecoveryOption.ExtendFinish; the detail text is minimal. The backend sends RecomputedPlan (src/types.ts:178-183) but the message shown (line 263) only shows the new finish date and remaining sections, not the impact on daily assignments.

**Section teaser is missing when plan_ready (no assignment yet)** — `major` · effort M
- *What breaks:* A reader opens a newly imported book on Today, sees the Plan Ready state, but no 'Before you read' teaser appears (TodayTeaser renders only when section exists, line 171). The teaser is a hand-written prompt that primes the reader for meaningful engagement—skipping it on first import loses a key touchstone for a deliberate reader. The 'Plan ready' copy (line 156) says 'Start today or begin tomorrow' but doesn't orient the reader to *what* they're about to read.
- *Fix:* When plan_status === 'plan_ready' and section is NOT null (the plan can still compute a first section even if not activated), render the teaser anyway—don't gate it on !planReady. Alternatively, when a new book is imported, compute and show a teaser for the *next* section that will be assigned once reading starts. This primes the reader without implying they're behind.
- *Evidence:* src/screens/Today.tsx:171 gates teaser on {section && <TodayTeaser/>}. In BookSetupSheet.tsx, 'Plan ready' is set at line 147, but no teaser preview is shown. The TodayTeaser (src/components/TodayTeaser.tsx) can render a teaser; the backend generates it (types.ts:230-239); but Today only renders it post-activation.

**Resume locator lost on a fresh plan_ready book that never gets read** — `minor` · effort S
- *What breaks:* If a reader imports a book, sets up the plan, returns to Today, then exits the app without reading, the next session resumes from the same spot (resume_locator). But if they DON'T return to Today on the same day (e.g., install the app, set up a book, close it, return in 3 days), the resume_locator is stale and points to the first section—the teaser and the reader still feel fresh, but the resume UX is silently wrong. This is a minor leak, but for a careful reader who delays first reading, it breaks the 'exactly where I left off' promise.
- *Fix:* When a plan is in plan_ready state and has never been activated (activated_at is null), do NOT preserve resume_locator from a prior attempted session. On first real activation, always start at the base offset of the assigned section (let the reader face the beginning of today's assignment). Resume only applies AFTER a plan is active and reading has begun.
- *Evidence:* src/screens/TextReader.tsx:162-163 uses today.resume_locator ?? makeCharLocator(baseOffset); this is safe. But src/screens/Today.tsx:114-115 checks resumePct >= 3 && resumePct < 97 to show the 'Continue' label; on a plan_ready book that has never been read, resume_percent could be set from an aborted attempt, and the label would lie.

**No explicit prompt to review/finalize notes when finishing a book** — `minor` · effort M
- *What breaks:* When a reader reaches the last section and clicks Finish (TextReader.tsx:1174), the recap panel offers 'Add a takeaway' (one final note), but there's no affordance to review the full Notebook before exiting the reader. A deliberate reader finishing a long book may want to glance at their notes, spot gaps, or add context before the session ends. The only way to review is to exit, go back to Today, click the Notes tab—which feels disconnected from the finishing moment.
- *Fix:* In the EndingPanel (recap), add a quiet 'Review notes for this book' link that opens the Notebook in a modal or slides it in alongside the recap (without closing the reader). Allow the reader to add final takeaways or reflections while still in the reading mindset. Only after review does Finish commit.
- *Evidence:* src/screens/TextReader.tsx:1165-1176 EndingPanel buttons; no notes preview. The NotesBrowser (src/screens/NotesBrowser.tsx) is a separate screen view, not accessible during the reader session.

**Margin tutor 'Go deeper' response can drop off-screen without scroll indication** — `minor` · effort M
- *What breaks:* A reader selects a passage, clicks 'Explain', gets a brief answer, then clicks 'Go deeper' to expand. The deeper response appends below (MarginTutorCard.tsx:18-21 describes this), but if the card is already near the bottom of the margin panel, the new content may scroll out of view with no visual cue that more text exists. A careful reader might miss the deeper explanation, thinking the 'Go deeper' click didn't work.
- *Fix:* When the 'Go deeper' response streams in and would push content off-screen, either (1) auto-scroll the margin panel to keep the new text visible, or (2) show a subtle 'Scroll down to see more' indicator. Preserve the brief answer in view (don't jump past it) so the reader sees the progression: brief → deeper.
- *Evidence:* src/components/MarginTutorCard.tsx:400+ (the deep-tier rendering logic); no scroll management for the appended response. The margin panel is a fixed-width scrollable aside (TextReader.tsx:678-686) but doesn't auto-scroll on new content.

**Forecast note on Today doesn't explain 'slightly_off_pace' clearly enough for decision-making** — `minor` · effort S
- *What breaks:* When forecast.state === 'slightly_off_pace', the forecastNote() shows 'Slightly off your original pace — a session today keeps the finish date within reach.' This is honest, but a deliberate reader planning their week needs to know: by how many days? Is it 1 day or 5? The note hides the projected_finish_date number, so the reader can't assess the actual impact. A careful strategist needs more data to decide if today's session is urgent or optional.
- *Fix:* When forecast state is 'slightly_off_pace', include the projected finish date and days_late: 'Slightly off pace — projected finish is DATE (X day(s) late). A session today narrows that to Y day(s).' This lets the reader make an informed choice about today without needing to open Settings or Notes.
- *Evidence:* src/screens/Today.tsx:24-32 forecastNote() function; the case for 'slightly_off_pace' shows only a generic message. The forecast object has projected_finish_date and days_late, but they're not rendered.

**Margin panel scroll doesn't reset when navigating to a new section** — `minor` · effort S
- *What breaks:* A reader is in the middle of a section, has scrolled the margin panel down to view notes, then clicks Next › to move to the next section. The margin notes change (new section's notes load), but the panel's scroll position stays at the bottom—the reader can't immediately see the first note of the new section. They must scroll back to the top manually. For continuous reading flow, this is a friction point.
- *Fix:* When currentIdx changes (section navigation), reset the margin panel's scrollTop to 0 so notes are immediately visible in order.
- *Evidence:* src/screens/TextReader.tsx:183-200 handles section loading; the margin panel scroll position (line 678-686, className tl-sidepanel) is not reset in the useEffect that loads a new section.
